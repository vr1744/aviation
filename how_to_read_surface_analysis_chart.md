# How to Read a Surface Analysis Chart

Surface analysis charts show current weather at the Earth's surface: pressure systems, fronts, winds, and observations.

## 1. Highs (H) and Lows (L)
- **H**: High pressure → calm, clear weather (clockwise winds in NH).  
- **L**: Low pressure → clouds, rain, wind (counterclockwise winds in NH).

## 2. Isobars
Lines of equal pressure. Close spacing = strong winds; wide = calm.

![Surface Chart Zoom with Isobars](https://pilotinstitute.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/Featured-Image_Surface-Analysis-Chart-Explained.jpg)  
![Annotated Isobars and Features](https://www.weatherhawks.com/content/images/2021/12/surf_analy_annotated-1.png)

## 3. Front Symbols
- Cold front (blue triangles)  
- Warm front (red semicircles)  
- Stationary (alternating)  
- Occluded (purple alternating)

![Front Symbols Legend](https://learntoflyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/11-25.png)  
![Full Graphical Legend](https://ocean.weather.gov/product_description/graph_prods_legend.gif)

## 4. Station Plots
Temperature, dew point, wind barbs, sky cover, weather symbols.

![Station Model Diagram](https://www.meteor.wisc.edu/~hopkins/aos100/stamod2.gif)  
![Buoy/Station Example](https://www.cfinotebook.net/graphics/weather-and-atmosphere/surface-analysis-chart/ship-buoy-plot-model-example.webp)

## Quick Tips
- Tight isobars + low + fronts → stormy.  
- Wide isobars + high → fair weather.  
- Always check valid time (UTC).
